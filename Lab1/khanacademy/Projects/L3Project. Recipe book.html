Elsweyr Fondue

  • Time: 30 mins
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Fondue:
Butter 200g
Shofrisk cheese 400g
Beer or ale 120ml
Moon Sugar Syrup:
Lavender or sumac 2 tbsp
Water 1 cup
Sugar 1 cup

Step 1: For the moon sugar syrup boil 1 cup of granulated white sugar in 1 cup of water. Stir until sugar dissolves. Remove from heat immediately and add lavender or culinary sumac to taste, usually 1 or 2 Tbsp. Let cool and use as you would in any simple syrup recipe.

Step 2: Melt a pat of butter in the top of a double boiler over medium heat. This is optional, but can help prevent sticking.

Step 3: When the butter foams, add the Snofrisk. Whisk slowly with the butter until well blended.

Step 4: Whisk in beer or ale, 4 oz. at a time or so, until the texture is thick and creamy, somewhere between the consistency of a melted milkshake and unwhipped whipping cream.

Step 5: Add the blue cheese in bits and gradually whisk in until smooth.

Step 6: Add a tablespoon or two of the moon sugar simple syrup. This will sweeten the fondue, so check to taste before adding a whole bunch of it.

Step 7: If your moon sugar simple syrup was on the mild side, add lavender or sumac a teaspoon at a time to adjust seasonings.

Step 8: If your fondue is a bit too thick, thin it with more beer or ale.

Step 9: Mess it forth into a pre-warmed earthenware bowl or your favorite fondue pot, and eat by dipping in sliced apples or chunks of lightly toasted bread!

Butterbeer

  • Time: 10 mins
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Drink:
Chilled cream soda 2l
Caramel extract 1/4 tsp
Butter extract 1/4 tsp
Cream Topping:
Heavy whipping cream 1 cup
Butterscotch topping 1/2 cup
Powdered sugar 1/4 cup

Step 1: In a large pitcher, mix the cream soda with the caramel and butter extracts. Set aside.

Step 2: In a large mixing bowl, use an electric mixer to whip heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks.

Step 3: Add butterscotch topping and powdered sugar. Mix to combine.

Step 4: Pour the soda mixture into clear cups or mugs. Pipe butterscotch cream topping over the butterbeer.

Step 5: Serve and enjoy!

Source: Favourite Family Recipes

Sweet Rolls

  • Time: 40 mins
  • Serves: 5
Ingredients Quantity
The Rolls:
Chicken eggs 5 eggs
Milk 1/4 cup
Flour 2 cups
Butter 1 cup
Vanilla extract 1 1/2 tsp
Granulated sugar 1 2/3 cups
The Glaze:
Butter 4 tbsp
Milk 2 tbsp
Powdered sugar 2 cups
Vanilla extract 1 tsp

Step 1: Gather your ingredients and allow to reach room temperature. Preheat your hearth to 350 degrees F.

Step 2: Cream the butter and sugar together and beat until fluffy (at least five minutes).

Step 3: Slowly add in each chicken's egg and beat well. Add the milk or cream.

Step 4: Add the flour, salt and vanilla extract and stir gently until mixed.

Step 5: Pour into greased swell roll pans and bake until golden, about 20 minutes. Allow to cool.

Step 6: For the glaze, melt the butter and add in the milk.

Step 7: Slowly mix in the powdered sugar and vanilla. Drizzle over cooled sweet rolls.

Step 8: Do not fight any dragons for 30 minutes after consuming more than 5 sweet rolls.
